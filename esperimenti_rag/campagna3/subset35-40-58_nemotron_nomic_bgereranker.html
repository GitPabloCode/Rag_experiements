Come si misura la distanza tra due BG consecutivi? Distance between balise groups is by definition the distance between closest balises of the two groups (i.e. between the Balise Reference Mark of the last one of the first group and the Balise Reference Mark of the first one of the second group). SS-040 12 Risposta: La distanza tra due BG consecutivi viene misurata come la differenza tra i valori di D_Est (posizione stimata) nei due punti di riferimento, poiché D_Est rappresenta la posizione più probabile del veicolo nel sistema di coordinate del veicolo al tempo specificato nel pacchetto di odometria, e la distanza percorsa tra due posizioni di traccia può essere calcolata come la differenza dei valori di D_Est nelle due posizioni.
Testo Originale: "- 12.3.1.1 The estimated distance, D_Est, shall be the most probable position of the vehicle in the vehicle coordinate system at the time given in the odometer packet, with reference to the vehicle position at the last reset of the odometry.\n- 12.3.1.2 Note: For any train movement, the most probable distance travelled between any two track positions can be computed as the difference between the measurement values of D_Est at the two positions.\n- 12.3.1.3 D_Max is defined as the most positive position of the vehicle in the vehicle coordinate system at the time given in the odometer packet, with all over- and under-reading amounts accumulated since the last reset of the odometry.\n- 12.3.1.4 D_Min is defined as the most negative position of the vehicle in the vehicle coordinate system at the time given in the odometer packet, with all over- and under-reading amounts accumulated since the last reset of the odometry." ## 4.1.1.6 Number of balises that can be processed per unit of time

Rule

Let 'd' be the distance run by a train at the maximum speed of the line during 0.8 s.

In this distance 'd', the number of encountered balises shall not exceed 8.

Note: The maximum speed of the line is the nominal line speed value (engineered SSP). Tolerances due to inaccuracy of speed measurements and speed margins before brake intervention are not to be taken into account for engineering.
